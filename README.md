# CIEGAS: Cyber-Informed Engineering (CIE) Tool for Gas Compressor Station Analysis

## Overview

The Cyber-Informed Engineering Gas Compressor Station Analysis Tool (CIEGAS) was developed in collaboration with the U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Cybersecurity, Energy Security, and Emergency Response (CESER). This tool enhances the security and resilience of gas compressor station installations by incorporating Cyber-Informed Engineering (CIE) principles into the deployment and operation.

## Systematic Approach

CIEGAS operates through a systematic, multi-step process designed to deliver a thorough engineering and cybersecurity analysis:

- **Analysis of System Services:** This initial step(s) involve a detailed selection and examination of the compressor station's operational functions, including compression, flow control, monitoring, and associated digital systems.
- **Consequence-Focused Analysis:** In this step(s), the tool guides a user through a CIE consequence analysis to assess the potential impacts of system failures or cyber incidents in relation to organizational risk tolerances, with consideration for safety, throughput, and reliability implications.
- **Cyber-Informed Engineering Mitigation Analysis:** In the final step(s), the tool guides a user through a CIE mitigation analysis to identify mitigation opportunities to consider for the compressor station.
